1998 Terrapin Invitational Tournament

The University of Maryland Academic Quiz Team announces that it will hold the Terrapin Invitational Tournament, now in its twelfth year at the College Park campus, on Friday, October 30, and Saturday, October 31, 1998.

Lord High Tournament Director of the Terrapin will be David Hamilton. Questions may be addressed by telephone to Dave at (301) 314-1153. E-mail inquiries (which are preferred) may be sent to dhamiltn@wam.umd.edu.

All regular teams are to be composed of either undergraduate students (full-time or part-time) or graduate students who attend the school for which they play. There is no limit on the number of graduate students allowed on a team. Masters and "bastard" teams are also welcome, but will be ineligible for the championship.

The fees are as follows:

  + $110 per team (don't be alarmed, there are plenty of discounts)
  + $10 late fee for questions arriving after 10/23 at 11:59:59 PM
  + $20 late fee for questions arriving after 10/27 at 11:59:59 PM
  + $20 for a copy of the rounds used in the 1998 Terrapin

  - $ 5 if your packet follows the ACF Nationals formatting guidelines
        (available upon request)
  - $10 maximum discount for a good packet

Questions should either be e-mailed as text or sent on disk as a Microsoft Word file (version 6.0 or higher) or in ASCII text.

We may be willing to extend credit based on the entrance fee to your tournament. We will trade rather than sell questions. Feel free to offer us deals.

In very rare cases, packet deadlines may be slightly negotiable.

What makes the Terrapin different from most other tournaments?

  • We offer a discount based on the quality of the packet submitted. We ask for a balanced packet of 25 toss-ups and 25 bonus questions in the ACF format. If more than 80% of your submitted questions are usable, you get $5 back. If more than 90% are usable, we refund $10. Minor editing (e.g., fixing typos, reordering phrases, etc.) and repeats will not count against you. It is hoped that a discount of this nature will encourage quality submissions; please read Marc Swisdak's ACF Question Writing Guidelines (available here) to assist you in preparing your packet.
  • Difficulty level will be between ACF Regionals and Nationals.
  • The Terrapin has consistently featured geographically diverse teams.
  • Tournament format will be round-robin. If bracketing is necessary, there will be a playoff round-robin similar to ACF Nationals.
  • Unlike most other tournaments, there will be trophies for the winning team and the runner-up.
  • Monetary awards can be given in cash or by check to avoid bureaucracy.
Question packets will be sold subject to the condition that they may not be placed on any archive site, photocopied, or redistributed without the express written authorization of the Maryland Academic Quiz Team for a period of one year.
