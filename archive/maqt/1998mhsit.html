1998 Maryland High School Invitational Tournament

The University of Maryland Academic Quiz Team is pleased to announce its seventh annual Maryland High School Invitational Tournament. This year, it will be held at the University of Maryland College Park on Sunday, November 22. Every team is guaranteed to play five games in a swiss-pair format, and the top 16 teams advance to a seeded elimination playoff.

The games will be played in a format similar to the It's Academic TV show, with some differences, to wit: the "letter-category" round that It's Academic abandoned a few years ago has been retained and the "visual" round found on the TV show has been replaced by a competitive category round in which all the answers have a common theme (e.g., U.S. Presidents). The rules of the game will be explained in more detail at the tournament, but depart in very few particulars from the It's Academic TV show.

Conferring between players is allowed at all times. You may have four players playing at any one time (not just three, though you may play short-handed). Alternates can be substituted between rounds, though not during games or between teams, if you bring multiple teams.

There are five rounds in each game: Letter Category, Untimed Individual, Category, Timed Individual, and Grab Bag. The rules for each round are summarized in the following table:

Round# Q'sUse buzzers?Points if correctPoints if incorrect
Letter Category8Yes+10-10
Untimed Individual7No+20- 0
Category10Yes+10-10
Timed Individual8No+20 *(see below)- 0
Grab Bag15Yes+20-20

(*) As on the It's Academic TV show, a team earns a 25 points bonus if it answers all 8 of its Timed Individual questions.

If buzzers are not used, the questions are read only to one team, so there are separate Untimed and Timed Individual rounds for each team. Each team is given 80 seconds to answer its 8 questions during the Timed Individual round.

To help clarify the above table and rules, an annotated round from a previous Maryland Invitational Tournament is available here.

The 64 team field will be divided into two brackets. The two brackets will alternate games, and if you have registered 2 teams, they will be placed in different brackets so that they can watch each other's games. Each team will play 5 preliminary games, making a total of 10 preliminary rounds.

There will be a break for lunch, during which you can find restaurants on Route 1 and in the Stamp Student Union. The preliminary rounds should end at approximately 3:00, with the top 16 teams advancing to a seeded playoff.

Trophies will be given to the first and second place teams. We will be selling the tournament questions on disk in either MAC or PC format for $10.

The easiest way to get to the University is via Route 1. From the Beltway, take exit 25 (Route 1) south towards College Park. About two miles down Route 1, you will pass a McDonald's on your right. Soon after that, Route 1 widens to three lanes. Get in the right lane and make a right at the first light, Campus drive. If you are coming from the south, you will pass the University Chapel on your left and Fraternity Row on your right, and then make a left turn onto Campus Drive. Once on Campus Drive, go straight ahead until you come to a traffic circle with a large M in the middle. Make a right turn onto Regents Drive. After you pass the Geology building you will see a large parking garage on your left. Park there at any metered spot. Meters are free on weekends, but do not park in any reserved space, as the department of campus parking is extremely vigilant. Across the street from where you entered the parking garage and slightly to the right is a low, brown sign with white letters that reads "Physics Lecture Halls". Enter through the doors of that courtyard and the lecture hall is to your left. There will be signs posted on the doors.

The cost is $40 for the first team you send, $35 for each additional team, minus a $5 discount per working buzzer system you bring (bring as many as you like). Please make checks payable to the Maryland Academic Quiz Team.

Please try to let us know by Monday, November 16 if you will be coming, and how many teams and buzzer systems you will bring. Because we use a swiss-pair system for preliminary games (we feel that this is the fairest method), we have a 64-team admissions cap. The sooner you respond, the more certain you are of entry into the tournament. To register via the WWW, you can use our online registration form. In addition, you can either mail the entry form available here, call our tournament coordinator, Josh Allen, at (301) 314-0020 or send email to jtallen@wam.umd.edu. You may pay by mail when you register, or at the tournament. Once you have registered, we will get you more information regarding times, location, maps, etc. Last year's Invitational drew 64 teams, and with your patronage, we hope this one will be as successful.

One last topic I'd like to mention: if anyone on your team is interested in continuing to play quiz bowl in college and is even remotely considering attending the University of Maryland, then we are very interested in them. Maryland has one of the top 3 college programs in the nation, and it is one of the deepest. We rely on good recruiting classes each year to continue our winning tradition. If any of your students are interested, have them talk to one of our team members at the tournament.

Sincerely,
